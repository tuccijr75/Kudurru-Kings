# Kudurru Kings — Economy & Rewards Doctrine v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical economy and reward structure for Kudurru Kings as a standalone game build. It governs:
- the dual-currency system
- card acquisition rules
- reward sources
- duplicate conversion
- apex-card progression
- collection growth pacing
- deckbuilding-economy interaction

This document is a product and progression doctrine. It does not replace gameplay rules.

---

## 1. Core economy doctrine

Kudurru Kings uses a **dual-currency system**:

1. **Kudurru Shards**
2. **Apex Sigils**

These currencies serve different purposes and must never collapse into a single undifferentiated grind economy.

### 1.1 Kudurru Shards
Kudurru Shards are the main earnable progression currency.

Use Kudurru Shards for:
- normal card acquisition
- faction collection growth
- starter deck unlocks
- non-apex crafting
- deck slots
- standard reward-track purchases
- normal shop offers
- standard progression sinks

### 1.2 Apex Sigils
Apex Sigils are the rare apex-progression currency.

Use Apex Sigils for:
- apex card progression
- apex unlock tracks
- ultra-fragment conversion steps
- apex vault access
- apex milestone claims
- elite reward ladders

Apex Sigils must remain scarce and meaningful.

---

## 2. Card rarity and acquisition structure

## 2.1 Standard rarity ladder
Standard collection tiers:
- Common
- Uncommon
- Rare
- SuperRare
- Legendary

These tiers belong to the normal collection economy and are primarily acquired through Kudurru Shards, rewards, progression, and normal card-unlock systems.

## 2.2 Ultra apex layer
Ultra is not a broad open rarity band. Ultra is an apex layer.

Canonical Ultra rule:
- **Anu** is an Ultra card
- **Adamu** is an Ultra card
- these are the **only Ultra cards**
- they are the **best cards in the game**
- they are **extremely difficult to obtain**

Ultra acquisition must never feel routine.

---

## 3. Dual-currency roles

## 3.1 Kudurru Shards role
Kudurru Shards should feel:
- steady
- reliable
- broadly useful
- visible in short play windows
- sufficient for normal collection growth

Kudurru Shards are the player’s ordinary progression engine.

## 3.2 Apex Sigils role
Apex Sigils should feel:
- rare
- prestigious
- milestone-based
- long-horizon
- never trivial

Apex Sigils are the gatekeeper currency for apex progression.

---

## 4. Reward sources

## 4.1 Kudurru Shards earn sources
Players earn Kudurru Shards through gameplay by:
- match completion
- win bonus
- first win of day
- daily quests
- weekly quests
- faction mastery milestones
- collection milestones
- rank track milestones
- achievement rewards
- duplicate conversion from non-apex cards
- event participation
- season progression

### Performance-linked shard bonuses
Kudurru Shard payouts may also scale modestly from:
- combat wins
- successful captures
- successful Arbitrations
- Site raids
- Boss Trial progress
- objective completion

This scaling must be modest. The game should reward meaningful play, not only raw time.

## 4.2 Apex Sigil earn sources
Players earn Apex Sigils through:
- long-form mastery tracks
- high-tier seasonal milestones
- elite achievement chains
- upper reward ladders
- special event milestones
- rare apex reward drops
- duplicate apex compensation
- visible pity/progress systems

Apex Sigils should not be part of the ordinary daily reward flow at high rates.

---

## 5. Ultra progression model

Ultra progression uses:
- **Apex Sigils**
- **Ultra Fragments**

This is the locked apex acquisition model.

## 5.1 Ultra Fragments
Ultra Fragments are card-specific apex progression pieces.

Canonical fragment rule:
- Anu uses **Anu Fragments**
- Adamu uses **Adamu Fragments**
- fragments are not generic between ultra cards
- fragments accumulate toward unlock and/or upgrade progression

## 5.2 Apex progression formula
Ultra acquisition requires:
- Apex Sigils
- Ultra Fragments
- a progression gate

This progression gate may include:
- mastery milestones
- account progression thresholds
- elite event completion
- season achievement completion

### Canon acquisition rule
Ultra cards may **not** be bought directly with Kudurru Shards.

### Canon unlock rule
An Ultra card is only unlocked when the player has:
- the required Apex Sigils
- the required Ultra Fragments
- the required progression gate completed

This keeps Ultra cards prestigious and non-trivial.

---

## 6. Currency sinks

## 6.1 Kudurru Shard sinks
Kudurru Shards are spent on:
- standard cards
- faction unlock bundles
- starter decks
- non-apex crafting
- deck slots
- normal progression offers
- future deckbuilder unlock functions

## 6.2 Apex Sigil sinks
Apex Sigils are spent on:
- apex unlock progress
- ultra-fragment conversion costs if used
- apex vault claims
- ultra acquisition milestones
- apex pity-track completion

Apex Sigils should not be spent on ordinary cards.

---

## 7. Duplicate handling

## 7.1 Standard duplicates
Duplicate Common, Uncommon, Rare, SuperRare, and Legendary cards convert into Kudurru Shards.

This conversion should:
- preserve progression value
- reduce bad-feel duplicate waste
- support future collection completion

## 7.2 Ultra duplicate handling
Ultra duplicate progression does not convert into normal Shards.

Ultra duplicate compensation should convert into one or more of:
- Apex Sigils
- Ultra Fragments
- apex pity progress

Ultra duplication must never cheaply destabilize apex rarity.

---

## 8. Pity and protection systems

## 8.1 Standard protection
The standard collection economy should include bad-luck protection so players can steadily complete ordinary sets over time.

## 8.2 Apex protection
Ultra acquisition must have a visible progress path.

Canonical apex protection rule:
- there must be **no infinite blind chase**
- every failed apex-acquisition attempt must contribute to eventual progress
- progress can be represented through:
  - Apex Sigil accumulation
  - Ultra Fragment accumulation
  - pity-track advancement
  - event milestone carryover

This makes Ultra cards hard, but not abusive.

---

## 9. Reward cadence

## 9.1 Match rewards
Every completed match should provide:
- base Kudurru Shards
- bonus for win
- small bonus for meaningful performance
- progress toward quests and mastery

## 9.2 Daily rewards
Daily structure should include:
- first win bonus
- daily quests
- rotating subsystem objectives
- rotating faction objectives

Example daily objectives:
- win 1 Arbitration
- perform 2 captures
- gain 3 Trial counters total
- win 1 match with a specified faction
- complete 3 matches

## 9.3 Weekly rewards
Weekly structure should provide:
- larger Kudurru Shard bundles
- long mastery progress
- small Apex Sigil payouts
- elite objective rewards

## 9.4 Mastery rewards
Faction mastery tracks should grant:
- Kudurru Shards
- faction cards
- cosmetics
- deck slots or deckbuilder unlock steps
- occasional Apex Sigils at major milestones

---

## 10. Deckbuilding economy rule

Custom deck rules will be defined later in Deck Builder doctrine, but the economy rule is locked now.

### Canon deckbuilding rule
Players spend currency to **acquire cards**, not to repeatedly pay for each deck edit.

This means:
- Kudurru Shards can unlock cards and deck-related access
- owned cards remain usable in deck construction
- building or editing a custom deck should not consume currency every time

This keeps deckbuilding ergonomic and avoids punishing experimentation.

---

## 11. Shop doctrine

## 11.1 Standard shop
The standard shop may sell:
- standard card offers
- starter deck bundles
- faction bundles
- standard crafting shortcuts
- cosmetics or presentation extras if later added

These should primarily use Kudurru Shards.

## 11.2 Apex shop or vault
A separate apex vault layer may exist for:
- apex progression offers
- Ultra Fragment bundles
- Apex Sigil sinks
- high-end milestone claims

This layer must remain heavily gated and slow.

---

## 12. Economy pacing targets

## 12.1 Kudurru Shards pacing
Kudurru Shards should support:
- visible short-session progress
- steady faction growth
- normal card acquisition without stagnation

## 12.2 Apex pacing
Apex progression should support:
- long-term chase
- visible but slow progress
- prestige without hopelessness

Apex cards should feel attainable only through committed play over time.

---

## 13. Launch doctrine for upper-level cards

Some upper-level cards beyond ordinary tiers may also be made available through gameplay progression systems rather than simple purchase.

Canonical rule:
- high-end card access should come from **play, mastery, and milestone achievement**
- not only from direct shop access
- apex cards must remain the hardest path

---

## 14. Anti-failure rules

Do not allow:
- Kudurru Shards to directly trivialize Ultra acquisition
- Apex Sigils to become routine daily filler
- apex cards to be random-only with no visible progress
- duplicate overflow to create runaway apex inflation
- deck editing to become a repetitive currency tax

---

## 15. Final doctrine

Kudurru Kings economy should create:
- steady standard collection growth
- strong faction progression identity
- visible reward for meaningful play
- long-term apex chase
- hard but fair acquisition of Ultra cards

The economy must make ordinary growth feel active and rewarding, while keeping apex cards rare enough to remain aspirational.
